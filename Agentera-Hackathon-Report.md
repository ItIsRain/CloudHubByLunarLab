### Overview

The MOEI AD42 Agentic AI Hackathon: Federal AI Services is a 48‑hour innovation challenge hosted at 42 Abu Dhabi, bringing together UAE-based university students to build next-generation agentic AI solutions for real federal energy and infrastructure services. Under the theme “Agentic AI for Federal Energy Infrastructure Services”, teams will design prototypes that can autonomously handle complete government workflows from input to outcome, with minimal human intervention.
Participants will work in teams of 3–5 to tackle pre-defined challenges, including smart asset inspection, federal housing concierge services, energy demand forecasting, executive country intelligence, AI-powered WhatsApp and call center agents, and digital assistants for MOEI’s website and mobile app. Each solution must demonstrate goal-driven autonomy, multi-step reasoning, integration with tools or systems, and measurable impact on real Ministry of Energy and Infrastructure processes, using synthetic or anonymized datasets that comply with UAE data and cybersecurity standards.
The hackathon is free to join and open to UAE residents aged 18+ who are currently enrolled in a UAE-based university or higher education institution. Over the course of the event, participants will receive mentorship, attend preparatory workshops, and compete for an AED 30,000 prize pool, internships, and special recognition awards, with judging by a mixed panel from MOEI, 42 Abu Dhabi, government, industry, and academia.

## 2. Challenge Tracks

### AI Agent for Rescheduling Housing Arrears

Build a specialized AI agent that instantly assesses housing arrears rescheduling requests — analyzing application data and supporting documents, verifying completeness, evaluating the beneficiary's financial situation, and proposing an appropriate rescheduling amount and duration based on approved governance rules. The agent should transform a process that today takes five working days into an instant, standardized service, while applying unified rules across all cases, automatically documenting the rationale behind each decision, and referring exceptional or high-risk cases to a human officer. Owned by MOEI's Finance and Collection Department in coordination with IT.

https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/11tKfR2e0PJGY_3urckc4jpTcJZXy2Pot

### AI-Powered Global Country Intelligence Platform

Develop an AI-powered executive intelligence platform that gives government leadership rapid, consolidated access to country profiles across energy, infrastructure, transport, sustainability, and economic sectors. The system should generate strategic country summaries (government structure, key initiatives, partnerships, national priorities, bilateral cooperation opportunities), surface real-time indicators (GDP, energy production, infrastructure indexes, competitiveness rankings), and auto-produce executive-ready briefing materials — including presentation slides, talking points, smart reports, and AI-generated short videos — to accelerate leadership preparedness for international meetings and visits.

https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1pfiuyLniktzPezZjgOzYbQWMQvlKaqvA

### Omnichannel AI Customer Engagement Agent

Build an Agentic AI system that unifies customer engagement across MOEI's three channels — WhatsApp, the voice contact center, and the website/mobile app — into a single intelligent layer. The agent should deliver cross-channel conversational intelligence (Arabic and English NLU, shared knowledge base, persistent customer context with seamless handover between channels), real-time voice sentiment analysis on calls, and automated operational execution (case creation, tracking, routing, and CRM-integrated resolution). The goal: consistent service quality, proactive engagement, and a unified real-time view of customer experience and operational performance for leadership.

- **Project Name:** Mizan - AI Loan Rescheduling Officer

**One-line Tagline:**

> A governed AI case officer that turns housing arrears rescheduling from a 5-day manual review into an instant, explainable, policy-compliant decision.

- **Track:** AI Agent for Rescheduling Housing Arrears

**Problem Statement:**

> Housing loan arrears rescheduling is currently handled after citizens are already financially distressed. The process depends on manual document review, salary and obligation checks, officer interpretation, financial study, repayment-capacity calculation, and recommendation drafting. This can take around five working days, creates heavy operational workload, and may lead to inconsistent decisions across similar cases.
> 
> The deeper issue is that much of the required data already exists across UAE PASS, MOEI systems, salary records, arrears history, payment history, and financial documents, but the decision workflow is still slow, reactive, and difficult to audit. Citizens see delayed outcomes, officers handle repetitive cases manually, and exceptional cases are not always separated clearly from straightforward cases.


**Solution Description:**

> Mizan is an autonomous AI case officer for Sheikh Zayed Housing Programme housing-loan arrears rescheduling. It is not a chatbot. It acts like a government officer that receives a rescheduling case, retrieves available profile and loan data, validates documents, studies income and obligations, applies approved rules, proposes a repayment path, explains the recommendation, and routes exceptional cases to a human officer.
> 
> The system uses a multi-agent workflow with an Orchestrator Agent, Document Auditor Agent, Affordability & Risk Agent, Policy Solver, Human Review Gate, Rationale Agent, and Audit Trail layer. Mizan enforces the core governance rules deterministically: the monthly deduction must not exceed 20% of beneficiary income, the repayment schedule must not exceed the original approved loan period, and active request conflicts are detected before a new decision is made.
> 
> Mizan supports multiple outcomes: approve rescheduling, request missing documents, maintain the current installment, transfer arrears to the end of the loan period, reject/block active duplicate requests, or escalate to an officer for suspicious, high-risk, hardship-sensitive, or low-confidence cases.
> 
> The prototype includes:
> 
> Citizen journey with synthetic UAE PASS login and case submission
> Officer dashboard for human-review cases
> Document validation and missing-document detection
> Deterministic policy engine for 20% deduction cap and repayment-period compliance
> Confidence scoring and escalation logic
> Bilingual English/Arabic transparency memo
> Full audit trail with rule IDs, evidence, timestamps, and reasoning
> Organizer-provided historical arrears data insights for risk calibration and realistic scenario design
> Proactive risk scanning to detect early arrears patterns before cases become severe
> 
> Mizan’s value is simple: clear cases can be processed instantly, complex cases stay under human control, and every recommendation is explainable, auditable, and aligned with governance.

**Submission** — status: `submitted` · submitted 2026-06-11 04:00 UTC

- **Project Name:** OmniMan
- **One-line Tagline:** Omnichannel intelligence for ministry services at scale
- **Track:** Omnichannel AI Customer Engagement Agent

**Problem Statement:**

> A single large model doing everything is the obvious approach — and the wrong one here. The problems:
> 
> It doesn't scale. One model holding every conversation becomes a bottleneck; concurrent voice calls queue behind each other, and you pay for a huge model even for trivial tasks like detecting "speak Arabic" or classifying intent.
> It's slow and expensive. Routing a one-word intent decision or transcribing audio through a frontier LLM wastes latency and cost on work a tiny specialized model does faster.
> It's stateful and fragile. Keep the big model "in" the call and a single crash drops the conversation; you can't elastically add capacity.
> It hallucinates across domains. A generalist gives shallow, sometimes wrong answers on housing fees vs. NOC rules vs. maritime permits, with no grounding.


**Solution Description:**

> Our solution: a distributed, specialized pipeline. Small models handle the minor parts — local ASR/TTS/SER for voice, a fast 2B router for intent — while the main LLM is invoked only per turn for synthesis, never tied up in a call.
